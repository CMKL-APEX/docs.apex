## Partition 
<br />
Partition is a form of resource management appropriate for the user's work type so that the program's performance suits the available resources. 
On the APEX system, it has been allocated in the following format:

<br />
---
| Partition	      | Node	| Time Limit	| CPU	| GPU	| RAM	| Max gpus per user  | Usage      |
| :-------------:	| :---:	| :------: 	  | :-:	| :-:	| :-:	| :-------------:    | :------:   |
|debug <Badge text="default" variant="success" />   |4	    |0:30:00	    |  2	| -	  | 8	  | <Badge text="No GPU" variant="danger" />  |Quick testing |
|cpu	            |4	    |168:00:00	  | 192	| -	  | 256	| <Badge text="No GPU" variant="danger" />	|CPU-only jobs |
|gpu	            |1	    |72:00:00	    | 64	| 4	  | 256	| 4	                 |Short tasks (1-4 GPUs) |
|batch	          |3	    |168:00:00	  | 128	| 8	  | 512	| 8	                 |LLM training (> 4 GPUs) |
|interactive	    |1	    |1:00:00	    | 16	| 1	  | 64	| 1	                 |Interactive jobs |

<br />

:::caution[Caution]
  If the user does not specify <Badge text="--partition=<Partition to use>" variant="note" />, the system will organize your Job/Task into the debug Partition by default.

:::
